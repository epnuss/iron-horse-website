We Work Directly
With Your Insurance.

Storm damage, wind damage, hail — if your roof took a hit, your homeowner's insurance may cover most or all of the repair. We've helped hundreds of South Florida homeowners navigate the claim process and get their roof replaced at little to no out-of-pocket cost.

  • Free damage inspection and documentation
  • We meet with your adjuster on-site
  • Detailed estimate submitted to your insurance
  • We handle the paperwork — you just approve the work
  • Supplement claims when insurance underpays

How the Insurance Process Works

1

Free Inspection

We inspect your roof and document all damage with photos for your claim.

2

We File the Claim

We help you file and meet your adjuster on-site to make sure nothing is missed.

3

Insurance Pays

Once approved, your insurer pays most of the cost. You pay only your deductible.

4

New Roof. Done.

What We Do

Every Roof Type.
Done Right.

From full replacements to emergency repairs — tile, shingle, flat, and everything in between.

🏠

Tile Roofing

Concrete and clay tile systems built for South Florida's wind loads and hurricane codes. Proper underlayment, mortar, and flashing — installed by the book, permitted and inspected.

🏡

Shingle Roofing

Architectural and impact-rated shingle systems from top manufacturers. Installed to Florida building code so your roof passes inspection and your insurance stays valid.

🏗️

Flat Roofing

TPO, modified bitumen, and torch-down for flat and low-slope roofs. Properly waterproofed, fully inspected — we don't leave until it's right.

🔧

Roof Repairs

Missing tiles, blown shingles, active leaks, storm damage — we diagnose fast and fix it right. Emergency response available when your roof can't wait.

🔍

Roof Maintenance

Annual inspections and preventative tune-ups that extend your roof's lifespan and catch problems before they become expensive emergencies.

📋

Wind Mitigation Reports

We provide wind mitigation inspections that can save you hundreds per year on homeowner's insurance premiums. Required by most FL insurers.
